Introduction

The Walking Dead is a compelling television series that delves into a post-apocalyptic world, where humanity struggles to survive in a desolate landscape overrun by zombies. Based on Robert Kirkman's eponymous comic series, the show follows a diverse group of survivors as they face not only the constant threats of the undead but also the internal and external challenges that arise in a world without rules or social structures. As characters evolve, the series explores themes such as survival, morality, and loyalty, offering an exhilarating and often heart-wrenching experience that has captivated audiences worldwide.

The Walking Dead premiered on October 31, 2010. It was exclusively broadcast on cable channel AMC in the United States and internationally through the Fox Networks Group and Disney+. The series concluded on November 20, 2022, after eleven seasons and 177 episodes. Andrew Lincoln played the lead character of Rick Grimes until his departure from the show in the ninth season.
